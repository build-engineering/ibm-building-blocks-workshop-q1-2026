TAX DOCUMENT REQUIREMENTS GUIDE

This guide outlines the required tax documents for different client types.

=== INDIVIDUAL CLIENTS ===

Required Documents (Standard):
1. W-2 Forms - Wage and Tax Statement from all employers
2. 1099 Forms - Income from freelance work, investments, or other sources
   - 1099-INT (Interest Income)
   - 1099-DIV (Dividend Income)
   - 1099-MISC (Miscellaneous Income)
   - 1099-NEC (Non-Employee Compensation)
3. 1098 Forms - Mortgage Interest Statement
4. Receipts for Deductible Expenses
   - Medical expenses (if itemizing)
   - Charitable contributions
   - State and local taxes paid
5. Prior Year Tax Return (for reference)
6. Social Security Numbers for all dependents
7. Bank Account Information (for direct deposit)
8. Health Insurance Form 1095-A, B, or C

Additional Documents (If Applicable):
- Student loan interest statements (1098-E)
- Tuition statements (1098-T)
- Childcare provider information
- Retirement account contributions (IRA, 401k)
- Property tax statements
- Business use of home expenses

=== SMALL BUSINESS CLIENTS ===

Required Documents (In Addition to Individual Requirements):
1. Schedule C - Profit or Loss from Business
2. Business Income Records
   - Sales receipts
   - Invoice records
   - Payment processor statements (PayPal, Stripe, etc.)
3. Business Expense Records
   - Office supplies
   - Equipment purchases
   - Software subscriptions
   - Professional services
   - Marketing and advertising
4. Mileage Log (if claiming vehicle expenses)
5. Home Office Documentation (if applicable)
6. Quarterly Estimated Tax Payment Records
7. Business Bank Statements
8. Depreciation Schedules for Assets
9. Inventory Records (if applicable)
10. Independent Contractor Payments (1099-NEC issued)
11. Business Insurance Policies
12. Business Licenses and Permits

=== CORPORATE CLIENTS ===

Required Documents (Comprehensive):
1. Form 1120 - U.S. Corporation Income Tax Return
2. Financial Statements
   - Balance Sheet
   - Income Statement (Profit & Loss)
   - Cash Flow Statement
3. General Ledger and Trial Balance
4. Payroll Records
   - W-2s for all employees
   - W-3 Transmittal
   - 941 Quarterly Federal Tax Returns
   - State payroll tax returns
5. Accounts Receivable and Payable Aging Reports
6. Bank Statements (all business accounts)
7. Loan Documents and Interest Statements
8. Depreciation Schedules
9. Asset Purchase and Disposal Records
10. Stock Transaction Records
11. Shareholder Information and K-1s
12. Prior Year Corporate Tax Returns
13. Estimated Tax Payment Records
14. State and Local Tax Returns
15. Sales Tax Returns (if applicable)
16. Property Tax Assessments
17. Insurance Policies and Premiums
18. Lease Agreements
19. Contract and Agreement Documentation
20. Board Meeting Minutes (if required)

=== IMPORTANT DEADLINES ===

Individual Filers:
- April 15 - Standard filing deadline
- October 15 - Extended filing deadline (if extension filed by April 15)

Small Business (Sole Proprietors):
- April 15 - Same as individual filers (Schedule C filed with Form 1040)
- Quarterly Estimated Taxes: April 15, June 15, September 15, January 15

Corporate Filers:
- March 15 - S-Corporations and Partnerships (Form 1120-S, 1065)
- April 15 - C-Corporations (Form 1120)
- September 15 - Extended deadline for S-Corps and Partnerships
- October 15 - Extended deadline for C-Corporations

=== DOCUMENT ORGANIZATION TIPS ===

1. Keep digital copies of all documents
2. Organize by category and year
3. Use clear, descriptive file names
4. Store securely with encryption
5. Maintain backups in multiple locations
6. Keep records for at least 7 years
7. Separate personal and business documents
8. Track document submission status
9. Set reminders for missing documents
10. Review completeness before deadline

=== COMMON QUESTIONS ===

Q: What if I'm missing a W-2 or 1099?
A: Contact the issuer immediately. If unavailable by filing deadline, use Form 4852 as a substitute.

Q: Can I file without all documents?
A: It's not recommended. Filing incomplete returns can lead to audits and penalties.

Q: What if I miss the deadline?
A: File as soon as possible. Late filing penalties apply, but they're less severe than not filing at all.

Q: Do I need receipts for all deductions?
A: Yes, keep receipts for all claimed deductions. The IRS may request proof during an audit.

Q: How long should I keep tax documents?
A: Generally 3-7 years. Keep indefinitely for property records and retirement accounts.

Q: What's the difference between filing status options?
A: Single, Married Filing Jointly, Married Filing Separately, Head of Household, and Qualifying Widow(er) have different tax rates and deduction limits.
